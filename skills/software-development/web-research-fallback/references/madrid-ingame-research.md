# Madrid In Game — Research Notes (May 2026)

## What is Madrid In Game?

Initiative of the **Ayuntamiento de Madrid** (Madrid City Council) to make Madrid the European capital of video games. Co-financed by the **European Union (NextGenerationEU)** and **red.es**.

### Three Centers (Campus del Videojuego — 3,000 m² in Casa de Campo)

1. **Experience Center**: Public-facing center with exhibitions, workshops, conferences. Home of "El Tardeo de Madrid in Game".
2. **Esports Center**: Professional esports training facility. Host of "Esports Series Madrid".
3. **Development Center**: Core of the entrepreneurship program. Runs a 3-phase program:
   - **Preincubation** (projects not yet launched)
   - **Incubation** (initial product versions, established company)
   - **Acceleration** (established company with metrics and recurring revenue)
   - Each phase lasts ~6 months
   - Includes mentorship, VR/AR/video/audio labs, networking with 70+ cluster companies, funding support

### The Cluster

One of Europe's largest digital ecosystems with **70+ gaming companies**. Members include:
- **EA, Microsoft, AWS, Deloitte, Globant, Mediatonic, Keywords Studios, ESL**
- **ADONIAS, MetaWorlds, Lab Cave, JuegaTerapia, IDG Consulting**
- **Infinity Talent, Vermila, ATLAS, KATAPULT, Wiper**
- **AEVI, DEV, Polo Digital, BM, GGTECH**
- **UCM (Universidad Complutense), ESNE, adigital**
- **ENSO, Voxel, L3TCraft, Madlions, EGEDA**

### Recent Achievements

- **GDC San Francisco**: 3 studios from the ecosystem selected among the 8 best indie games in the world ("Best in Play" award)
- **4YFN (MWC)**: Accelerator startups showcased at the global startup stage
- **Londres**: Strengthened international positioning with European gaming leaders
- **FITUR**: Strategic collaboration lines with BA in Game
- **BIG Bilbao & Gamergy IFEMA**: Closed 2025 with major industry fairs

### Industry Data

- Global gaming market: **$185+ billion annually**
- Spain: **10th largest market worldwide**, ~$1.5 billion annual revenue
- Spain: **40% of population** plays video games
- Gaming is the fastest-growing entertainment industry (70% growth potential vs. 17% general entertainment)
- Recognized as a cultural industry by the Spanish Congress of Deputies (unanimous 2009 resolution)

### Key Trends (Observatorio Gaming)

- Mobile Games global revenue: **$81.5 billion in 2025** (+0.2% YoY)
- **AI generative** revolutionizing game development (Google Project Genie AI, etc.)
- **Xbox Helix Project**: New console allowing PC games
- **Therapeutic gaming**: Games for elderly, mental health applications
- Gaming for education and gamification in public administration
